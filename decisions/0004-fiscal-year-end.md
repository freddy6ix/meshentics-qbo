# ADR-0004 — Fiscal year-end: July 31

- **Status:** Recommended (not locked until first T2 filed; Mike validates at year-end)
- **Date:** 2026-06-04 EDT
- **Decider:** Frederick delegated the choice to the bookkeeping agent ("no preference… take your recommendation").
- **Supersedes:** the tentative "June 30" selected during the 2026-06-04 intake.

---

## Context

Meshentics was incorporated **2025-08-15** (federal CBCA). A corporation's **first tax
year cannot exceed 53 weeks** (≈371 days) from incorporation, so the first fiscal
year-end must fall on or before ~2026-08-21. The year-end is not legally fixed until the
first T2 corporate return is filed, so this remains adjustable with Mike at year-end.

## Decision

Adopt a **July 31 fiscal year-end.** First fiscal year: **2025-08-15 → 2026-07-31**
(~11.5 months).

## Rationale

- **Longest permissible first year.** July 31, 2026 is the latest clean month-end inside
  the 53-week limit (Aug 31 would breach it). A longer first period keeps all startup
  costs and early activity in one return and tells a cleaner first-year story.
- **Maximum deferral.** First T2 filing deadline is 6 months after year-end →
  **2026-07-31 → T2 due 2027-01-31.** (Balance of tax, if any, due 2–3 months after
  year-end depending on CCPC/SBD status — Mike confirms; likely a loss year regardless.)
- **Avoids the Dec-31 personal-tax crunch** and gives the catch-up modestly more runway
  (year-end ~57 days out vs. 26 under June 30).
- **Low-risk / reversible** — not locked until the first T2; Mike validates.

## Consequences

- Catch-up and first-year close target **2025-08-15 → 2026-07-31** for corporate
  activity; pre-incorporation activity (from first spend in **May 2025**) is brought on
  as startup costs via the shareholder loan within FY1 (see [company-profile.md](../company-profile.md)).
- Federal **annual return** (corporate-law filing, not tax) is separate, on the
  08-15→10-14 window — unaffected by this tax year-end choice.
